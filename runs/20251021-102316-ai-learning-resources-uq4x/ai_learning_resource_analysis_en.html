Method summary

Six-week learning spine

To give the team a structured progression, the following six 5/5 recommendations balance agentic workflows, Claude API engineering, developer productivity, retrieval augmentation, system architecture, and mathematical intuition.

  1. AI Agents in LangGraph — Clusters: Agentic AI & multi-agent systems, Generative AI development & productization — Why it matters: DeepLearning.AI short course co-taught by Harrison Chase and Tavily, demonstrating LangGraph agent workflows spanning planning, tool use, reflection, memory, and human-in-the-loop patterns.
  2.   3. Generative AI for Software Development — Clusters: Generative AI development & productization, Developer productivity & tooling — Why it matters: Laurence Moroney's three-course certificate embedding LLM pair programming, testing, dependency management, database design, and design patterns into a modern software workflow.
  4.   5. Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) — Clusters: RAG & retrieval augmentation — Why it matters: DeepLearning.AI's Retrieval-Augmented Generation course covering embeddings, indexing, reranking, prompt orchestration, and evaluation notebooks.
  6. 3Blue1Brown — Clusters: Mathematics & theoretical foundations, ML & deep learning fundamentals — Why it matters: 3Blue1Brown channel with animated explanations of linear algebra, calculus, neural networks, and transformers.

Tag heat interpretation

Transformer (4), LangChain (3), LangGraph (2), and RAG (2) dominate the list, signalling a strong appetite for LangChain-powered agentic systems. Meanwhile Notebook hands-on, Structured outputs, and Role specification reflect daily prompt craft. MCP integration, Security governance, and Deployment strategy emphasise production readiness. Consider pairing a weekly “Prompt & MCP lab” with end-to-end RAG deliverables so practice reinforces architecture patterns.
